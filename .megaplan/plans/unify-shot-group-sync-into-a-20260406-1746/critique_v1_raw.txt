        # Implementation Plan: Unify Shot Group Sync — Stale Video Detection

## Overview
Currently, shot groups in `mode:'video'` have no mechanism to detect when a newer final video becomes available after the group was switched to video mode. The image-mode sync (`usePinnedGroupSync`) only handles `mode:'images'` groups. The `useStaleVariants` system handles individual clip variant staleness but doesn't understand shot groups or final videos.

**Goal:** Extend the shot group system to detect stale videos and surface an "Update to latest video" action — without modifying `usePinnedGroupSync`'s image sync logic or `useStaleVariants`.

**Approach:** Rather than putting video staleness detection inside `usePinnedGroupSync` (which is an effect-based auto-sync hook), the simpler approach is to compute `staleShotGroupIds` as a derived value in `TimelineEditor.tsx`. The detection is a pure comparison — no debouncing or side effects needed. This avoids complicating the sync hook and keeps the data flow straightforward.

**Key insight:** When `switchToFinalVideo` creates a video clip, it stores `generationId: finalVideo.id` on the asset entry, and `videoAssetKey` on the pinned group. Staleness = `registry[group.videoAssetKey].generationId !== finalVideoMap.get(group.shotId).id`.

## Main Phase

### Step 1: Compute stale shot group IDs in TimelineEditor (`TimelineEditor.tsx`)
**Scope:** Small
